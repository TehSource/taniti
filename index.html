About the Island

Taniti is a small Pacific island under 500 square miles with sandy and rocky beaches, a safe harbor, lush rainforests, and a mountainous interior featuring a small active volcano. The island’s indigenous population is about 20,000, and until recently the economy centered on fishing and agriculture.

Sightseeing

Most visitors spend time in Taniti City along Yellow Leaf Bay’s white beaches. Popular activities include boat or bus tours, rainforest hikes, and visiting the volcano.

Good to Know
